Jet lag

The body clock did not read the itinerary.

Jet lag is what happens when the airline says you have arrived, but your internal organs are still somewhere over the ocean, filling in a complaint form.

What helps?

  • Get daylight at the new destination when possible, because your body clock is old-fashioned and still respects the sun.
  • Avoid planning anything heroic for the first evening, unless “heroic” means finding soup and going to bed.
  • Use local meal times as gentle signals, even if your stomach is operating on a different legal system.
  • Keep caffeine useful rather than theatrical; coffee is a tool, not a personality.
