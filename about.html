About the clock

A world clock with opinions.

WhatTime Is It? began with a simple thought: when you are making tea, finishing work, or pretending not to be tired, someone elsewhere is already starting tomorrow with frightening confidence.

This site uses your browser timezone to show your local time, then compares it with cities around the world. It does not need your exact GPS location, and it does not need to know what street you are on, which is a relief for everyone involved.

The aim is to make time zones more memorable, slightly funnier, and more shareable than the usual wall of clocks that looks as if it belongs in an airport control room.

Why it exists

Time zones are useful, but they are also faintly ridiculous. This site turns them into tiny observations about work, sleep, lunch, travel, and the universal human tendency to check the fridge again.

What comes next

Possible future ideas include printable certificates, city-specific humour, travel product guides, and a “Mother-in-Law Approved” section for people who enjoy practical advice delivered with unnecessary authority.
